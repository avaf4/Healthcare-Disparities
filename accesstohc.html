Inaccessibility in Healthcare

"The real reason American health care is so expensive"

Costs

The elevated costs of doctor's visits, hospital visits, medicine, medical procedures, etc. keep many Americans from seeking the health assistance they need. 1 in 10 Americans don't have any form of health insurance which greatly adds to medical costs. With the lack of a national healthcare system, these uninsured and disadvantaged Americans turn to cheaper means to take care of any health problem (i.e. using ibuprofen for pain, using home remedies for a persistent cough, and many other things).

Location

Those who live in rural areas live farther away from medical centers than those who live in cities or suburbs. Those who live in rural areas are also more likely to lack health insurance and are usually older. These two groups of people are at a higher risk of lacking proper healthcare. Those in cities are also at risk since they are more likely to live in poverty which hinders access to healthcare.
